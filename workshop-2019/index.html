DASH IF Workshop on Media Streaming Meets 5G

Public Workshop co-sponsored by DASH-IF

Dec 9-10, 2019 - Portland, OR, USA

MISSION AND GOAL

Along with its 22nd face to face meeting on December 10-12 in Portland, OR, the DASH Industry Forum (DASH-IF) will use this opportunity to organize a workshop in order to gather experts from video distribution services, mobile operators and technology providers. With first 5G deployments kicking off, this is an opportunity to understand some of the technical and non-technical challenges and opportunities for mobile video services. Such information gathering allows companies and standardization organizations to focus on relevant enablers in order to create revenue-generating and interoperable video services, anticipating that video already is and will be to an increasing degree the majority of the traffic in mobile networks. Experts from different fields will discussed during two days on new opportunities and challenges. The goal of the workshop is to identify the challenges and possible gaps for fully taking advantage of 5G networks for multimedia streaming and related applications.

REGISTRATION

DASH-IF cordially invites you to attend its workshop on technical opportunities and challenges of media streaming applications over 5G networks. Registration is possible here https://dashif5g2019.eventbrite.com.

PROGRAM

The workshop is setup by a couple of introductory talks and deep dives on several enabler technologies that relate to mobile distribution in general and 5G specifically.

Introductory Talks (each 30min): * Overview of 5G Systems and Technology (Thomas Stockhammer, Qualcomm) * Overview of 5G Deployments and Business Models (Operator or Analysist) * Overview of User Studies/Analysis (Amir Gomroki, Ericsson) * Overview of latest Media Streaming Developments (John Simmons, Microsoft)

Deep Dive Technologies with connection to 5G including discussion leaders) * New codecs (Thierry Fautier, Harmonic) * Content-Aware Encoding (Yuriy Reznik, Brightcove) * Impact of new protocols (Ali Begen, Comcast) * Ingest technologies (Rufael Mekuria, Unified Streaming and Imed Bouazizi, Qualcomm) * Low-Latency streaming challenges and opportunities (Nicolas Weil, Amazon Elemental) * Ad Insertion (Iraj Sodagar, Tencent) * Content Protection (Laurent Piron, Nagra) * Broadcast/multicast and hybrid delivery (Thomas Stockhammer, Qualcomm) * Cloud computing, edge computing, and media on cloud (Imed Bouazizi, Qualcomm) * AR/VR/MR and other interactive applications (Thierry Fautier, Harmonic) * Device platform diversity and hybrid DASH/HLS (Cedric Thienot, Enesys tbc and Thomas Stockhammer, Qualcomm)
